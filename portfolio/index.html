About

Over six years I have worked as the analytical backbone for Growth and Product orgs — from defining activation metrics and experiment programs to co-owning pricing transitions and company-wide health scorecards. That work trained me to translate messy reality into metrics people trust, and narratives executives act on.

The through-line in my recent chapter is AI: not as a slide bullet, but as production systems — agents on top of product and customer data that answer recurring questions autonomously, reduce load on analysts, and raise the bar for self-serve insight. I partner deeply with engineering on retrieval, evaluation, and guardrails so analytics stays accurate and explainable.

Across analytics, product partnership, and data-intensive problem solving, I default to the same habits: crisp problem framing, causal caution, alignment with engineering and GTM — and, where it helps, AI as a way to scale answers without diluting trust in the underlying metrics.
